Density-based clustering: A powerful tool for solving architectural problems, particularly in spatial analysis, urban planning, and design optimization. By identifying areas of high density and spatial relationships, architects can make data-driven decisions to enhance functionality, sustainability, and aesthetics.

Building Energy Efficiency:
Problem: Optimize energy consumption in large commercial or residential buildings.
Application: Cluster zones within a building based on energy usage patterns (e.g., lighting, HVAC). Identify underused areas that can benefit from automated lighting or temperature control systems.

Smart buildings in New York City have used DBSCAN to segment areas with excessive HVAC demands for targeted retrofitting.

Construction Material Optimization:
Problem: Reduce material waste on construction sites.
Application: Cluster areas where similar materials are required, such as concrete or steel reinforcements. Plan batch deliveries to these clusters for just-in-time inventory management.

Lendlease used clustering techniques for material logistics on large-scale projects.

Historical Site Preservation:
Problem: Preserve the integrity of heritage sites while accommodating modern usage.
Application: Cluster areas of high visitor density to minimize wear and tear on fragile structures. Redesign pathways or restrict access based on density clusters.

Machu Picchu employed clustering to manage tourist movement and protect the site.
